## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Full Size Kegerators

Choosing the right full-size kegerator involves more than just picking the one that looks best. To ensure you get a machine that meets your needs for years to come, consider these crucial factors before making your purchase.

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations
First, think about what kind of beer you'll be serving. If you're a fan of major domestic brands like Coors or Miller, you'll need to check for compatibility, as their kegs are often oversized. Homebrewers will need a kegerator that can accommodate multiple Cornelius (corny) kegs. Also, consider where you'll use it. If it's for an outdoor patio, you'll need a model specifically rated for outdoor use, like the HCK Kegerator, which can handle temperature fluctuations and humidity. For indoor use, noise level and aesthetics become more important.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($X-$Y ranges)
Full-size kegerators can range in price from around $400 for a basic, entry-level model to over $1,500 for a premium, multi-tap, outdoor-rated unit.
*   **Entry-Level ($400 - $600):** These models, like the EdgeStar KC2000, offer reliable cooling and basic features. They are perfect for beginners or those with a limited budget.
*   **Mid-Range ($600 - $900):** In this range, you'll find models with features like digital temperature controls, stainless steel finishes, and broader keg compatibility, such as the EdgeStar KC3000SS.
*   **Premium ($900+):** These are top-of-the-line units, often featuring multiple taps, outdoor ratings, and built-in installation options. They are designed for serious enthusiasts and entertainers.

### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Look beyond the basics. A **digital thermostat** offers far more precision than a manual dial. A **deep chill** or **quick cool** function is invaluable for chilling a new keg quickly. The number of taps is also crucial; a **single tap** is fine for one dedicated beer, but a **dual or triple tap** system offers variety for you and your guests. Also, check if the unit comes with a complete dispense kit, including a CO2 tank, regulator, and coupler.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
The longevity of your kegerator depends on its build quality. Look for models with a **stainless steel door** for durability and ease of cleaning. An **internal steel floor plate**, like the one in the Kegco model, is a huge plus as it prevents damage from heavy kegs. Check the quality of the included components like the tower, faucets, and regulator. Higher-quality components will provide a better pour and last longer.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Stick with well-known brands like EdgeStar, Kegco, and HCK. These companies have a proven track record of producing reliable products and generally offer better customer support and warranty coverage. Reading user reviews can provide insight into a brand's long-term reliability and how they handle any potential issues. A good warranty (e.g., 1 year on parts, 3+ years on the compressor) is a sign that the manufacturer stands behind their product.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your available space carefully before buying. Note the kegerator's dimensions, including the height with the tap tower installed and the depth with an external CO2 tank, if applicable. If you plan to move the unit around for parties or cleaning, ensure it comes with **casters (wheels)**. A **reversible door** can also offer more flexibility for placement in tight spaces.

## Is it A Wise Decision to Buy full size kegerators?

Investing in a full-size kegerator is a significant decision, but for any true beer aficionado, the benefits often far outweigh the initial cost. The primary value proposition is the unmatched freshness and taste of draft beer. Beer from a keg is not exposed to light or excessive oxygen, preserving its flavor exactly as the brewer intended. This is a taste experience that bottles and cans simply cannot replicate.

From a financial perspective, buying beer by the keg is almost always more cost-effective than buying it in smaller packages. Over time, the savings on beer can pay for the kegerator itself, especially if you entertain frequently or have a household of beer drinkers. A standard half-barrel keg holds the equivalent of 165 12-ounce beers, and the per-pint cost is significantly lower.

Beyond taste and cost, a kegerator is an impressive centerpiece for any home bar, game room, or man cave. It elevates your hosting game, providing a unique and enjoyable experience for guests. Compared to the alternative of a cooler full of melting ice and cans, a kegerator is more convenient, less messy, and more environmentally friendly by reducing packaging waste. While the upfront investment is notable, the long-term benefits of superior taste, cost savings, and elevated entertaining make a full-size kegerator a very wise decision for those who appreciate great beer.

## What Is the Best Way to Choose full size kegerators?

Choosing the best full-size kegerator is a process of matching your specific needs with the right features. Following a structured approach can prevent buyer's remorse and ensure you get the perfect unit for your home.

**Step 1: Assess Your Primary Use.**
Are you a homebrewer needing space for corny kegs? An entertainer who needs multiple taps? Or a casual drinker who just wants a single, reliable domestic beer on tap? Your answer will guide your decision on capacity, tap configuration, and keg compatibility.

**Step 2: Define Your Space and Budget.**
Measure the area where the kegerator will go. Decide if you need a freestanding model or one that can be built into cabinetry. Set a realistic budget based on the price ranges discussed earlier. This will narrow down your options significantly.

**Step 3: Prioritize Key Features.**
Create a "must-have" list. For some, this might be a digital thermostat for precision. For others, it could be an outdoor rating or a stainless steel finish. Decide what features are non-negotiable and which are "nice-to-haves".

**Step 4: Research and Read Reviews.**
Use this guide as a starting point. Look into the recommended brands and models. Read user reviews on retail sites to understand the real-world performance and potential quirks of each unit. Pay attention to comments about noise level, ease of setup, and customer service.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   **Ignoring Keg Size:** Don't assume all kegerators fit all kegs. Specifically check for compatibility with oversized kegs like those from Coors and Miller if you plan to serve them.
*   **Forgetting Hidden Costs:** Remember that the included CO2 tank will ship empty. Factor in the cost of getting it filled locally.
*   **Choosing Looks Over Function:** A stylish kegerator is great, but if it can't hold a consistent temperature, it's useless. Prioritize cooling performance and build quality first.

**When to Upgrade vs. Budget Options:**
A budget option like the EdgeStar KC2000 is perfect if you are new to kegerators or have simple needs. It's reliable and gets the job done. You should consider upgrading to a premium model like the Kegco or HCK if you demand precise temperature control, need multiple taps for variety, require outdoor durability, or want a unit that will be a long-term centerpiece of your entertainment space.

## FAQ

### What is the best full size kegerators for beginners?
For beginners, the **EdgeStar KC2000 Full Size Kegerator** is an excellent choice. It is affordable, easy to assemble, and provides reliable cooling without complicated controls. Its ability to convert into a standard refrigerator adds extra value, making it a low-risk investment for someone just starting with home draft beer systems. It includes all the essential components to get you started.

### How much should I spend on full size kegerators?
You can expect to spend between $400 and $900 for a quality full-size kegerator. Entry-level models are in the $400-$600 range and are great for basic needs. Mid-range models from $600-$900 offer better features like digital controls and stainless steel finishes. Premium, multi-tap, or outdoor-rated models can exceed $1,000. Set your budget based on the features that are most important to you.

### What features are most important in full size kegerators?
The most important features are consistent temperature control, keg compatibility, and build quality. A reliable cooling system is non-negotiable for good beer. Ensure the unit fits the type of kegs you plan to use. Look for durable construction, such as a reinforced floor and quality tap components, to ensure longevity and a good pouring experience.

### How do I maintain my full size kegerators?
Regular maintenance is key. Clean the beer lines every two to three weeks or with every keg change using a kegerator cleaning kit. This prevents bacteria buildup and ensures your beer tastes fresh. You should also regularly wipe down the interior, check CO2 levels, and ensure the condenser coils on the back are free of dust for efficient operation.

### Are expensive full size kegerators worth it?
Expensive kegerators are worth it if you will use their advanced features. Premium models offer more precise temperature control, better insulation for energy efficiency, higher quality components that last longer, and options like multiple taps or outdoor ratings. If you are a serious beer enthusiast or entertain often, the investment in a higher-end model often pays off in performance and durability.

### What brands make the best full size kegerators?
EdgeStar, Kegco, and HCK are among the most reputable brands in the full-size kegerator market. EdgeStar is known for offering a wide range of models with great features at various price points. Kegco is highly regarded for its durable construction and high-quality components. HCK specializes in premium, versatile models, including excellent outdoor-rated options.

### How long do full size kegerators typically last?
A well-maintained full-size kegerator can last for 10 to 15 years, or even longer. The lifespan depends on the quality of the components, particularly the compressor, and how well you maintain the unit. Regular cleaning of the unit and its lines, along with ensuring proper ventilation for the compressor, will significantly extend its life.

### Can I find good full size kegerators on a budget?
Yes, you absolutely can. Models like the **EdgeStar KC2000** or the **VEVOR Dual Tap Kegerator** offer excellent performance and features at a budget-friendly price. While they may lack some premium features like digital controls, they are more than capable of delivering cold, fresh draft beer. They provide a fantastic entry point into the world of home dispensing.
