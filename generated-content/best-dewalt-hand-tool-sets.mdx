Choosing the right DEWALT tool set involves more than just picking the one with the most pieces. To make a wise investment, consider your specific needs, the features that matter most, and the overall value.

**What is DirectTorque Technology?**
DirectTorque Technology is a DEWALT socket design feature. Instead of applying force to the fragile corners of a nut or bolt head, it applies force to the flat sides. This spreads the pressure over a larger area, significantly reducing the chance of 'rounding off' the fastener, which can be a major headache. It also allows for greater torque application and a more secure grip.

**What is the difference between 6-point and 12-point sockets?**
6-point sockets have six points of contact inside the socket and are the standard for most mechanical work. They offer a better, more secure grip on hexagonal fasteners and are less likely to slip or strip a bolt, especially when applying high torque. 12-point sockets have twelve points and can be useful for 12-point fasteners or in very tight spaces where getting the socket onto the bolt head is difficult, but they have a higher risk of rounding off a standard 6-point bolt.
